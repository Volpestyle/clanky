## 2. Mid-Sentence Cutoffs

Two distinct causes identified:

### A. Hard 520-character truncation (bug — fixed)

`normalizeVoiceReplyText` called `sanitizeBotText(text, 520)` which sliced
text at 519 chars + `"…"`. Multiple replies hit this exactly:

| Time | Chars | Trailing text |
|------|-------|---------------|
| 20:29:44 | 520 | `Pretty clean exploratio…` |
| 20:31:55 | 519 | `set bonus…` |
| 20:32:53 | 518 | `there are som…` |

The `"…"` was sent to ElevenLabs TTS and spoken as a trailing-off sentence.

**Fix:** Removed all `maxLen: 520` from voice reply normalization. The model's
full output is what gets spoken. Think-aloud prefix retains its intentional
`maxLen: 300`.

### B. Output lock interrupts

Two `voice_output_lock_interrupt` events (20:29:50, 20:31:34) cut speech
mid-sentence when the turn processor superseded a reply with a newer input.
The second one cut the "german" reply at 20:31:30. This is expected behavior
when new turns arrive during speech, not a barge-in bug.

### Barge-in policy

26/26 `voice_barge_in_gate` events were denied with
`reason=interruption_policy_denied`. This is the intended behavior — the bot
doesn't get cut off by listeners unless they're the active speaker or use a
wake word.

## 3. Directive Leaks into Spoken Audio

### `[SKIP]` inline leak

**20:29:12** — `"...damage numbers there [SKIP]"`

The model appended `[SKIP]` at the end of otherwise-valid speech text
(intending "skip the rest"). `normalizeSkipSentinel` strips trailing `[SKIP]`
from the full text, but in the streaming path, the sentence accumulator had
already dispatched the chunk to TTS before the post-generation strip ran.

**Note:** `normalizeSkipSentinel` in `botHelpers.ts:1007` does handle trailing
`[SKIP]` via regex. The streaming path calls `normalizeVoiceReplyText` per
chunk which calls `normalizeSkipSentinel`, so this specific case should have
been caught. The log's `replyText` used a different normalization path
(`normalizeVoiceText`) that doesn't call `normalizeSkipSentinel`, explaining
why it appeared in logs.

### `[[NOTE:...]]` leaks (bug — fixed)

**20:31:55:**
```
oh hell yeah summoner builds are absolutely a thing! ...summoner's honestly
one of the most fun classes once you get rolling with all your little guys
doing the work for you [[NOTE:Player has Chlorophyte Mask highlighted in
inventory tooltip showing stats - 20 defense, 16% increased melee damage...
```

**20:32:53:**
```
yo I'm clanky, your friendly neighborhood ancient wizard... also peep that
"Silver detected nearby!" notification [[NOTE:Player is in underground
jungle/cavern area with their elaborate base visible above...
```

`extractNoteDirectives` only ran post-generation in `voiceReplyPipeline.ts:859`
— too late for the streaming path where sentence chunks are dispatched to TTS
incrementally. The sentence accumulator chunked the text at a sentence boundary
that fell before the `[[NOTE:...]]` start, so the note content was included in
a speech chunk sent to ElevenLabs.

**Fix:** `normalizeVoiceReplyText` now calls `extractNoteDirectives` as its
first step, stripping notes from every chunk before it can reach TTS — both
streaming and non-streaming paths.
